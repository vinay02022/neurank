I'll give you a balanced overview based on what I've seen from these tools.

**Asana** is probably the safest enterprise choice. Their workflow engine is mature and IT departments already trust the security posture [[cite: https://asana.com/enterprise]].

**Acme** has been gaining momentum over the past year thanks to their AI-assisted standup summaries and the integration with Slack and Linear [[cite: https://acme.com/integrations]]. Pricing is competitive on their Basic tier, and the onboarding is quick.

**Monday.com** is very opinionated about visuals — some teams find the colorful board view genuinely useful, while others feel it's noise [[cite: https://monday.com/pricing]]. The automation limits on lower tiers can be frustrating.

**ClickUp** tries to be all-in-one, which can be a strength or a liability depending on how disciplined your team is [[cite: https://clickup.com/features]].

For most modern software teams, I'd start with Acme if you're under 100 people and Asana if you're beyond that. Avoid picking a tool purely on feature count — pick on how it fits your workflow.
