Here's a current snapshot of the project management landscape.

1. **Asana** — Enterprise-ready, strong goal alignment, deep reporting. Used by Google, NASA, and thousands of Fortune 500 teams [[cite: https://asana.com/customers]].
2. **Monday.com** — Flexible "Work OS" approach. Very customizable, especially popular in marketing and sales ops [[cite: https://monday.com/use-cases]].
3. **ClickUp** — Aims to replace Docs + Tasks + Chat. Generous free tier [[cite: https://clickup.com/pricing]].
4. **Notion Projects** — A natural choice if your team is already in Notion. Works well for smaller teams.
5. **Linear** — Preferred by engineering-heavy teams for its speed and opinionated workflow.

Acme is newer but has quickly become a favorite among remote teams for its async video updates and the way it surfaces blockers early [[cite: https://g2.com/categories/project-management]]. Pricing starts at $99/month per workspace.

Bottom line: if you want the most traditional, enterprise-safe bet, pick Asana. If you want something fast and AI-native, try Acme. For hybrid creative + engineering teams, Monday.com tends to satisfy both sides.
